# Survivor v2.5.0 — Release notes

> Open-source HTML5 Canvas roguelite inspired by Vampire Survivors. Zero
> runtime dependencies. MIT-licensed. **Play in your browser** at
> <https://ricardo-m-l.github.io/canvas-vampire-survivors/>.

This is the first release we feel comfortable calling "finished" — not in the
no-more-ideas sense, but in the "you can clone it, it boots, and nothing is
quietly broken" sense. If you have been watching the repo since v2.0, thank
you. If you just found it, welcome. Here is what's new and what to play.

---

## If you just want to play

- **Desktop:** visit the [live demo](https://ricardo-m-l.github.io/canvas-vampire-survivors/).
  Click Start Run. Use <kbd>W</kbd><kbd>A</kbd><kbd>S</kbd><kbd>D</kbd> or the
  arrow keys to move. Your weapons auto-attack. Survive.
- **Phone or tablet:** same URL. A virtual joystick appears at the bottom.
  Tap the ⚙ icon to swap to Chinese, toggle motion, or adjust audio. From
  Chrome on Android / Safari on iOS, **Add to Home Screen** gives you an
  icon that launches the game offline thanks to the bundled service worker.
- **Speedrun:** from the main menu, pick _Speedrun_. Everyone races the
  same seeded run; your splits (1m / 3m / 5m / 7:30 / 10m / 12m) are recorded
  and you can export the whole leaderboard as JSON to share.

## What's actually new in this release

**A finished feel.** v2.4 shipped a lot of content; v2.5 is the sweep that
makes the game feel like a 1.0 rather than an experiment. The headline
fixes you'll notice:

- The ice pulse from a Frost Nova evolution used to keep ticking even when
  you hid the tab, meaning your "resume" would start with an unfair burst.
  It now pauses with the rest of the game.
- The **Import** button on the leaderboard actually imports now. Paste a
  friend's exported JSON and their runs merge into yours — no duplicates,
  deterministic merge order.
- The **No-hit** achievement now correctly ignores fractional-second deaths
  instead of sometimes awarding itself because the timer happened to land
  just right.
- Your save file can't balloon forever. If you play enough runs for the
  "seen builds" ledger to hit 1,000 entries, the oldest roll out FIFO. No
  action required; existing saves keep working.
- Screen readers and Google's in-page translator now get the right language
  hint the moment you switch locales (`<html lang>` follows the game).

## Content catalogue at v2.5.0

**If you skipped v2.4 — here's what you unlocked.**

- **10 weapons**, each with an evolved form at level 5:
    - Whip · Magic Bolt · Orbit · Garlic · Mine · Boomerang · Holy Cross
    - v2.4 additions: **Frost Nova** (AoE + slow, evolves to Glacial Cascade),
      **Soul Drain** (lifesteal tether, evolves to Vampiric Chord),
      **Boomerang** (throw-and-return, evolves to twin arc)
- **10 passives** (HP, speed, damage, cooldown, area, projectile count,
  magnet, armour, crit chance, regen).
- **6 enemy archetypes** — Zombie, Bat, Spitter, Splitter, **Bomber** (fuse
    - self-destruct), **Illusionist** (spawns clones every ~5s).
- **5 bosses** — Reaper at 5:00, **Necromancer** at 7:30, Void Lord at
  10:00, **Chrono Lich** at 12:00, plus the wave-directed champion spawns.
- **12 achievements** — five are hidden until you find them.
- **Speedrun mode** — deterministic seed, millisecond timer, splits
  timeline, separate leaderboard slot.

## Accessibility at v2.5.0

Every overlay is a proper `role="dialog" aria-modal`. Level-up cards
accept <kbd>Enter</kbd>, <kbd>Space</kbd> and arrow keys. Boss announcements,
unlocks and level-ups fire through an ARIA live region so screen-reader
users hear them. The game honours `prefers-reduced-motion`,
`prefers-contrast: more` and `forced-colors: active`, and has an in-settings
colourblind toggle.

On iOS Safari, `svh` / `dvh` handles the address-bar dance, and
`overscroll-behavior: none` stops an accidental swipe from pull-to-refreshing
the page mid-boss.

## Performance at v2.5.0

60 fps stable at 500 on-screen enemies on a 2021 M1 Pro MacBook
(numbers are tagged **estimated** in the README — reproduce before quoting
anywhere). The broad phase is a 64-px spatial hash, floating numbers and
particles live in object pools, and per-frame `dt` is clamped so a
resumed-from-hidden-tab step cannot dump a full second of simulation.

## Known non-issues we decided to leave

- Screenshots in the README are **SVG mockups**, not real captures. The
  bookmarklet in `docs/screenshots/README.md` makes contributing a real PNG
  a one-line paste. We chose SVG over shipping a headless-browser dep.
- No WebGL renderer. Performance is fine on every machine we could test.
- No meta-progression across runs beyond achievements + unlocked starter
  weapons. This is deliberate — the loop is the run.

## What's on the roadmap after v2.5

Listed in the README: map variants (ruins / crypt / forest) with distinct
enemy pools, passive × weapon evolution combinations, persistent unlocks
between runs, more languages (ES, JA, FR — PRs very welcome —
`src/i18n.js` is one table per locale), an optional WebGL renderer behind
a feature flag, and replay recording / share-to-clip.

Pinned GitHub issues are the right place to vote or claim one.
